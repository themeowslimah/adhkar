Item 1 — The Virtue of Seeking Knowledge
الأَحَادِيثُ — اُنْظُرْ وَاقْرَأْ وَتَدَبَّرْ

Workbook p.10 — three hadiths on the merit of seeking knowledge.

١

«مَنْ سَلَكَ طَرِيقًا يَلْتَمِسُ فِيهِ عِلْمًا، سَهَّلَ اللَّهُ لَهُ بِهِ طَرِيقًا إِلَى الجَنَّةِ»

«Man salaka ṭarīqan yaltamisu fīhi ʿilman, sahhala-llāhu lahu bihi ṭarīqan ila-l-jannah»

"Whoever travels a path seeking knowledge upon it, Allāh makes easy for him by it a path to Paradise."

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
The reward is tied to the journey, not just the destination. Every step taken toward learning — opening a book, the walk to a class, even the search for a teacher — is itself counted as a step toward Paradise. This reframes the effort of study as worship, so no day of sincere seeking is ever wasted, even one where you feel you grasped little.
٢

«مَنْ يُرِدِ اللَّهُ بِهِ خَيْرًا، يُفَقِّهْهُ فِي الدِّينِ»

«Man yuridi-llāhu bihi khayran, yufaqqih-hu fi-d-dīn»

"Whoever Allāh wishes good for, He grants him understanding of the religion."

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺمُتَّفَقٌ عَلَيْه · Bukhārī & Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Being granted real fiqh — depth of understanding, not just memorised facts — is named here as a sign that Allāh wills good for you. That makes the love of learning a gift to be grateful for, and gives a quiet self-check: a settled aversion to understanding one's religion is something to be concerned about, not normalised.
٣

«إِذَا مَاتَ الإِنْسَانُ انْقَطَعَ عَنْهُ عَمَلُهُ إِلَّا مِنْ ثَلَاثَةٍ: إِلَّا مِنْ صَدَقَةٍ جَارِيَةٍ، أَوْ عِلْمٍ يُنْتَفَعُ بِهِ، أَوْ وَلَدٍ صَالِحٍ يَدْعُو لَهُ»

«Idhā māta-l-insānu-nqaṭaʿa ʿanhu ʿamaluhu illā min thalāthah: illā min ṣadaqatin jāriyah, aw ʿilmin yuntafaʿu bih, aw waladin ṣāliḥin yadʿū lah»

"When a person dies, his deeds come to an end except from three: an ongoing charity, knowledge that is benefited from, or a righteous child who supplicates for him."

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Death closes the ledger of deeds — except for three doors you can prop open now: charity that keeps giving, knowledge others keep using, and a righteous child who keeps praying for you. Teaching, writing, planting, and raising children well are all "ongoing charity." It is an invitation to build things whose reward outlives you.
🌱 Word root
سَلَكَ · salaka
From س-ل-ك — to travel, to take a path. The hadith promises that taking the physical and intellectual road of learning is itself a road Allāh smooths toward Paradise. Yaltamis (يَلْتَمِس) = he seeks out, gropes for.

Item 2 — The Virtue of Learning the Qurʾān
الأَحَادِيثُ — اُنْظُرْ وَاقْرَأْ وَتَدَبَّرْ

Workbook pp.12–13 — three hadiths on the merit of the Qurʾān.

١

«خَيْرُكُمْ مَنْ تَعَلَّمَ القُرْآنَ وَعَلَّمَهُ»

«Khayrukum man taʿallama-l-qurʾāna wa ʿallamah»

"The best of you are those who learn the Qurʾān and teach it."

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺرَوَاهُ البُخَارِيّ · al-Bukhārī

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Notice the measure of "best": not the most eloquent or wealthy, but the one who both learns and gives. Knowledge of the Qurʾān is only complete when it flows onward; hoarding it falls short of the praise. Even teaching one verse to one person places you in this hadith.
٢

«مَثَلُ الَّذِي يَقْرَأُ القُرْآنَ وَهُوَ حَافِظٌ لَهُ مَعَ السَّفَرَةِ الكِرَامِ البَرَرَةِ. وَمَثَلُ الَّذِي يَقْرَأُ وَهُوَ يَتَعَاهَدُهُ وَهُوَ عَلَيْهِ شَدِيدٌ، فَلَهُ أَجْرَانِ»

«Mathalu-lladhī yaqraʾu-l-qurʾāna wa huwa ḥāfiẓun lahu maʿa-s-safarati-l-kirāmi-l-bararah. Wa mathalu-lladhī yaqraʾu wa huwa yataʿāhaduhu wa huwa ʿalayhi shadīd, fa-lahu ajrān»

"The one who recites the Qurʾān and has memorised it is with the noble, righteous scribe-angels. And the one who recites it, persevering at it though it is hard for him, has two rewards."

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺرَوَاهُ البُخَارِيّ · al-Bukhārī

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
A gift for the struggling reader: the fluent memoriser keeps the company of angels, yet the one who stumbles and perseveres earns two rewards — one for the recitation, one for the difficulty. So a halting, error-prone recitation is not a lesser worship; the effort itself is separately rewarded. Never let "I'm not good at it" keep you from the Qurʾān.

📖 السَّفَرَة the angels · الكِرَام those honoured with Allāh · البَرَرَة those obedient to Allāh · يَتَعَاهَدُهُ he keeps reviewing & perfecting it.

٣

«اقْرَؤُوا القُرْآنَ، فَإِنَّهُ يَأْتِي يَوْمَ القِيَامَةِ شَفِيعًا لِأَصْحَابِهِ»

«Iqraʾu-l-qurʾāna, fa-innahu yaʾtī yawma-l-qiyāmati shafīʿan li-aṣḥābih»

"Recite the Qurʾān, for it will come on the Day of Resurrection as an intercessor for its companions."

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
The relationship is built now, claimed later. The Qurʾān becomes an intercessor specifically for "its companions" — those who kept its company through regular recitation. The question it quietly asks: on that Day, will the Qurʾān recognise you as a friend it spent time with?
⭐ Two kinds of reciter, both rewarded
The fluent memoriser is ranked with the angels; the one who struggles through it earns two rewards — one for reciting and one for the effort. Difficulty in learning the Qurʾān is never wasted.

Item 3 — The Virtue of Remembrance (Dhikr)
الأَحَادِيثُ — اُنْظُرْ وَاقْرَأْ وَتَدَبَّرْ

Workbook pp.14–15 — three hadiths on the merit of remembering Allāh.

١

«مَثَلُ الَّذِي يَذْكُرُ رَبَّهُ وَالَّذِي لَا يَذْكُرُ رَبَّهُ، مَثَلُ الحَيِّ وَالمَيِّتِ»

«Mathalu-lladhī yadhkuru rabbahu wa-lladhī lā yadhkuru rabbah, mathalu-l-ḥayyi wa-l-mayyit»

"The likeness of the one who remembers his Lord and the one who does not is like the likeness of the living and the dead."

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺمُتَّفَقٌ عَلَيْه · Bukhārī & Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Dhikr is presented not as an optional extra but as the very pulse of a living heart. A person can be biologically alive yet spiritually inert; remembrance is what keeps the heart awake to Allāh. The remedy for a heavy, distracted heart is rarely complicated — it is to start the tongue and mind moving in His remembrance again.
٢

«يَقُولُ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى: أَنَا عِنْدَ ظَنِّ عَبْدِي بِي، وَأَنَا مَعَهُ إِذَا ذَكَرَنِي. فَإِنْ ذَكَرَنِي فِي نَفْسِهِ ذَكَرْتُهُ فِي نَفْسِي. وَإِنْ ذَكَرَنِي فِي مَلَأٍ ذَكَرْتُهُ فِي مَلَأٍ خَيْرٍ مِنْهُمْ»

«Yaqūlu-llāhu taʿālā: Anā ʿinda ẓanni ʿabdī bī, wa anā maʿahu idhā dhakaranī. Fa-in dhakaranī fī nafsihi dhakartuhu fī nafsī. Wa in dhakaranī fī malaʾin dhakartuhu fī malaʾin khayrin minhum»

"Allāh the Most High says: I am as My servant thinks of Me, and I am with him when he remembers Me. If he remembers Me within himself, I remember him within Myself; and if he remembers Me in a gathering, I remember him in a gathering better than it."

حَدِيثٌ قُدْسِيّ — قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺمُتَّفَقٌ عَلَيْه · Bukhārī & Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Two life-changing principles in one ḥadīth. First: think well of Allāh — expect His mercy, forgiveness, and response, and you meet Him as you expected. Second: remembrance is reciprocated and upgraded — remember Him privately and He remembers you; remember Him before people and He mentions you before the angels, a gathering "better than it." Your small dhikr is answered with something greater.
٣

«سَبَقَ المُفَرِّدُونَ»، قَالُوا: وَمَا المُفَرِّدُونَ يَا رَسُولَ اللَّهِ؟ قَالَ: «الذَّاكِرُونَ اللَّهَ كَثِيرًا وَالذَّاكِرَاتُ»

«Sabaqa-l-mufarridūn», qālū: wa ma-l-mufarridūna yā rasūla-llāh? Qāl: «Adh-dhākirūna-llāha kathīran wa-dh-dhākirāt»

"The mufarridūn have gone ahead." They asked: "And who are the mufarridūn, O Messenger of Allāh?" He said: "The men and women who remember Allāh abundantly."

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
There is a quiet race to Allāh, and its frontrunners are not the most famous or accomplished — they are those, men and women alike, who simply remember Allāh a great deal. It is a wonderfully accessible kind of greatness: available on a commute, in a queue, while doing chores. Abundance of dhikr is how anyone can "go ahead."
💡 "I am as My servant thinks of Me"
This ḥadīth qudsī invites you to hold a good opinion of Allāh — to expect His mercy and acceptance. And remembrance is reciprocal: remember Him quietly, He remembers you; remember Him among people, He mentions you among a better assembly (the angels).

Item 4 — The Virtue of Gatherings of Remembrance
الحَدِيثُ — اُنْظُرْ وَاقْرَأْ وَتَدَبَّرْ

Workbook p.16 — the reward of those who gather to recite and study the Qurʾān.

١

«وَمَا اجْتَمَعَ قَوْمٌ فِي بَيْتٍ مِنْ بُيُوتِ اللَّهِ، يَتْلُونَ كِتَابَ اللَّهِ، وَيَتَدَارَسُونَهُ بَيْنَهُمْ، إِلَّا نَزَلَتْ عَلَيْهِمُ السَّكِينَةُ، وَغَشِيَتْهُمُ الرَّحْمَةُ، وَحَفَّتْهُمُ المَلَائِكَةُ، وَذَكَرَهُمُ اللَّهُ فِيمَنْ عِنْدَهُ»

«Wa ma-jtamaʿa qawmun fī baytin min buyūti-llāh, yatlūna kitāba-llāh, wa yatadārasūnahu baynahum, illā nazalat ʿalayhimu-s-sakīnah, wa ghashiyat-humu-r-raḥmah, wa ḥaffat-humu-l-malāʾikah, wa dhakarahumu-llāhu fī man ʿindah»

"No people gather in one of the houses of Allāh, reciting the Book of Allāh and studying it together, except that tranquillity descends upon them, mercy envelops them, the angels surround them, and Allāh mentions them to those (angels) who are with Him."

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
This is the strongest encouragement to never study alone-by-default. A circle of Qurʾān draws down four descending gifts at once — calm, mercy, encircling angels, and Allāh's mention of you on high. The lesson is practical: prioritise gatherings of learning and remembrance; what you "gain" there is far more than information.

📖 السَّكِينَة = calm & dignity (aṭ-ṭumaʾnīnah wa-l-waqār) · غَشِيَتْهُم = it covered them · حَفَّتْهُم = they encircled them.

⭐ Four gifts at every circle of knowledge
Notice the four things promised to a study-circle of the Qurʾān: tranquillity descends, mercy covers, angels surround, and Allāh mentions them above. A single ḥadīth — and a complete encouragement to attend gatherings of remembrance and learning.

Item 5 — Entering the Privy
الذِّكْرُ — اِحْفَظْ وَاعْمَلْ

Workbook p.20 — what the Prophet ﷺ said upon entering the place of relieving oneself.

٥

«اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الخُبُثِ وَالخَبَائِثِ»

«Allāhumma innī aʿūdhu bika mina-l-khubuthi wa-l-khabāʾith»

"O Allāh, I seek refuge in You from the male devils and the female devils."

كَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ إِذَا دَخَلَ الخَلَاءَ قَالَمُتَّفَقٌ عَلَيْه · Bukhārī & Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Islam sanctifies even the most mundane, exposed moments. The privy is a place where one's ʿawrah is uncovered and devils linger; so the believer crosses its threshold under Allāh's protection. The principle is bigger than the bathroom: begin every vulnerable moment by seeking refuge in Allāh, and no part of your day is left outside His remembrance.

📖 الخُبُث = the male devils · الخَبَائِث = the female devils · يُقَالُ عِنْدَ إِرَادَةِ دُخُولِ مَكَانِ قَضَاءِ الحَاجَةِ (said when about to enter).

💡 Why seek refuge here?
The privy is a place where the shayāṭīn are present. The Prophet ﷺ taught us to enter under Allāh's protection — a reminder that even our most ordinary, private moments begin with remembrance of Him.

Item 6 — Leaving the Privy

«غُفْرَانَكَ»

«Ghufrānak»

"(I seek) Your forgiveness." (i.e. "O Allāh, forgive me.")

كَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ إِذَا خَرَجَ مِنَ الغَائِطِ قَالَرَوَاهُ أَحْمَد · Aḥmad

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
A beautiful turn of the heart: having just been relieved of bodily discomfort, the believer's first thought is to seek relief from the burden of sin. Some scholars add that one asks forgiveness for the lapse in remembering Allāh while inside. The deeper habit it trains is gratitude that immediately becomes humility — every relief is a reminder of the Reliever.
🌱 Word root
غُفْرَانَك · ghufrānak
From غ-ف-ر — to forgive, to cover. The meaning is "O Allāh, forgive me." Scholars note its beauty: having just been relieved of bodily harm, the believer turns to ask to be relieved of the harm of sin.

Item 7 — After Completing Wuḍūʾ
الذِّكْرُ — اِحْفَظْ وَاعْمَلْ

Workbook p.22 — the testimony said after a thorough wuḍūʾ, and its reward.

٧

«مَا مِنْكُمْ مِنْ أَحَدٍ يَتَوَضَّأُ، فَيُبْلِغُ - أَوْ: فَيُسْبِغُ - الوُضُوءَ، ثُمَّ يَقُولُ: أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، وَأَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُ اللَّهِ وَرَسُولُهُ. إِلَّا فُتِحَتْ لَهُ أَبْوَابُ الجَنَّةِ الثَّمَانِيَةُ، يَدْخُلُ مِنْ أَيِّهَا شَاءَ»

«Mā minkum min aḥadin yatawaḍḍaʾu, fa-yublighu — aw: fa-yusbighu — l-wuḍūʾa, thumma yaqūl: ashhadu an lā ilāha illa-llāh, wa anna Muḥammadan ʿabdu-llāhi wa rasūluh. Illā futiḥat lahu abwābu-l-jannati-th-thamāniyah, yadkhulu min ayyihā shāʾ»

"There is none among you who performs wuḍūʾ thoroughly and then says: 'I bear witness that none has the right to be worshipped but Allāh, and that Muḥammad is His servant and Messenger,' except that the eight gates of Paradise are opened for him to enter through whichever he wishes."

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Two small habits, an enormous return. Completing wuḍūʾ properly (not rushing past the heels and elbows) and then sealing it with the shahādah opens all eight gates of Paradise — you choose your entrance. The lesson: the everyday acts we treat as routine are, when done with care and presence, among the heaviest deeds on the scale.

📖 يُسْبِغُ الوُضُوءَ = he completes it in the most perfect manner.

⭐ Eight gates for a few words
A complete wuḍūʾ followed by the shahādah opens all eight gates of Paradise. Never rush wuḍūʾ, and never skip this short testimony afterward — it is among the easiest, weightiest deeds.

Item 8 — The Call to Prayer (Adhān)
الأَذْكَارُ — اِحْفَظْ وَاعْمَلْ

Workbook pp.24–26 — what to say while hearing the adhān, and the duʿāʾ after it.

١

«إِذَا سَمِعْتُمُ المُؤَذِّنَ فَقُولُوا مِثْلَ مَا يَقُولُ، ثُمَّ صَلُّوا عَلَيَّ» — وَيَقُولُ عِنْدَ الحَيْعَلَتَيْنِ: «لَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ»

«Idhā samiʿtumu-l-muʾadhdhina fa-qūlū mithla mā yaqūl, thumma ṣallū ʿalayy» — wa yaqūlu ʿinda-l-ḥayʿalatayn: «Lā ḥawla wa lā quwwata illā bi-llāh»

"When you hear the muʾadhdhin, repeat what he says, then send blessings upon me." — but at "ḥayya ʿala-ṣ-ṣalāh / ḥayya ʿala-l-falāḥ" one says: "There is no might nor power except with Allāh."

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
The call to prayer is not just a clock — it is an invitation you verbally accept. By echoing the muʾadhdhin you answer the summons, and at "come to prayer / come to success" you confess your own helplessness: only Allāh's strength gets you up and to the prayer. The reframing turns a background sound into a moment of real connection five times a day.
٢

«مَنْ قَالَ حِينَ يَسْمَعُ المُؤَذِّنَ: أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ، وَأَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ، رَضِيتُ بِاللَّهِ رَبًّا، وَبِمُحَمَّدٍ رَسُولًا، وَبِالإِسْلَامِ دِينًا؛ غُفِرَ لَهُ ذَنْبُهُ»

«Man qāla ḥīna yasmaʿu-l-muʾadhdhin: ashhadu an lā ilāha illa-llāhu waḥdahu lā sharīka lah, wa anna Muḥammadan ʿabduhu wa rasūluh, raḍītu bi-llāhi rabban, wa bi-Muḥammadin rasūlā, wa bi-l-islāmi dīnā; ghufira lahu dhanbuh»

"Whoever says when he hears the muʾadhdhin: 'I bear witness that none has the right to be worshipped but Allāh alone, without partner, and that Muḥammad is His servant and Messenger; I am pleased with Allāh as Lord, with Muḥammad as Messenger, and with Islām as religion' — his sin is forgiven."

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ — بَعْدَ فَرَاغِ المُؤَذِّنِ مِنَ الشَّهَادَتَيْنِرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
The word that earns the forgiveness is "raḍītu" — "I am pleased." It is a renewal of contentment with the three anchors of a life: Allāh as your Lord, Muḥammad ﷺ as your guide, Islām as your way. Said sincerely five times a day, it slowly re-aligns a restless heart to be at peace with its Lord and its path.
٣

«اللَّهُمَّ رَبَّ هَذِهِ الدَّعْوَةِ التَّامَّةِ، وَالصَّلَاةِ القَائِمَةِ، آتِ مُحَمَّدًا الوَسِيلَةَ وَالفَضِيلَةَ، وَابْعَثْهُ مَقَامًا مَحْمُودًا الَّذِي وَعَدْتَهُ» — حَلَّتْ لَهُ شَفَاعَتِي يَوْمَ القِيَامَةِ

«Allāhumma rabba hādhihi-d-daʿwati-t-tāmmah, wa-ṣ-ṣalāti-l-qāʾimah, āti Muḥammadan-i-l-wasīlata wa-l-faḍīlah, wa-bʿathhu maqāman maḥmūdan-i-lladhī waʿadtah» — ḥallat lahu shafāʿatī yawma-l-qiyāmah

"O Allāh, Lord of this perfect call and the prayer about to be established, grant Muḥammad the means (al-wasīlah) and the high station, and raise him to the praiseworthy rank that You promised him." — (Whoever says it) my intercession becomes due for him on the Day of Resurrection.

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ — بَعْدَ الأَذَانِرَوَاهُ البُخَارِيّ · al-Bukhārī

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Notice the selflessness this duʿāʾ trains: in the gap between adhān and iqāmah — a time when supplication is answered — you are taught to ask first for the Prophet ﷺ, that he be given the highest rank. The reward loops back to you: his intercession on the Day you will most need it. Caring for him secures his care for you.

📖 الحَيْعَلَتَانِ = "ḥayya ʿala-ṣ-ṣalāh" & "ḥayya ʿala-l-falāḥ" · الوَسِيلَة = a rank in Paradise for only one servant — hoped to be the Prophet ﷺ · المَقَام المَحْمُود = the great intercession to relieve mankind at the Reckoning.

Item 9 — Entering & Leaving the Masjid
الأَذْكَارُ — اِحْفَظْ وَاعْمَلْ

Workbook p.27 — what to say on entering and on leaving the masjid.

١

«إِذَا دَخَلَ أَحَدُكُمُ المَسْجِدَ فَلْيَقُلِ: اللَّهُمَّ افْتَحْ لِي أَبْوَابَ رَحْمَتِكَ. وَإِذَا خَرَجَ فَلْيَقُلِ: اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ مِنْ فَضْلِكَ»

«Idhā dakhala aḥadukumu-l-masjida fa-l-yaqul: Allāhumma-ftaḥ lī abwāba raḥmatik. Wa idhā kharaja fa-l-yaqul: Allāhumma innī asʾaluka min faḍlik»

"When one of you enters the masjid let him say: 'O Allāh, open for me the gates of Your mercy.' And when he leaves let him say: 'O Allāh, I ask You from Your bounty.'"

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
The two duʿāʾs map the two halves of life. Entering the house of worship you ask for mercy — the currency of the Hereafter. Leaving for the marketplace of the world you ask for bounty — lawful provision. Islam does not split "religious" from "worldly": you carry remembrance across the threshold in both directions, asking for the right thing in each domain.

Item 10 — The Opening Supplication
الأَذْكَارُ — اِحْفَظْ وَاعْمَلْ

Workbook pp.28–31 — three authentic forms of the duʿāʾ said silently after the opening takbīr, before the Fātiḥah. Vary between them.

١

«سُبْحَانَكَ اللَّهُمَّ وَبِحَمْدِكَ، وَتَبَارَكَ اسْمُكَ، وَتَعَالَى جَدُّكَ، وَلَا إِلَهَ غَيْرُكَ»

«Subḥānaka-llāhumma wa bi-ḥamdik, wa tabāraka-smuk, wa taʿālā jadduk, wa lā ilāha ghayruk»

"Glory be to You, O Allāh, and praise; blessed is Your name; exalted is Your majesty; and there is no god but You."

كَانَ عُمَرُ ﷺ يَسْتَفْتِحُ بِهِمُسْلِم وَالدَّارَقُطْنِيّ · Muslim & Dāraquṭnī

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Before asking Allāh for anything, you first honour Him — glorifying, praising, declaring His name blessed and His majesty exalted. It is the etiquette of approaching a King: praise opens the door before requests are made. Starting the prayer this way settles the heart out of the world and into His presence.
٢

«اللَّهُمَّ بَاعِدْ بَيْنِي وَبَيْنَ خَطَايَايَ كَمَا بَاعَدْتَ بَيْنَ المَشْرِقِ وَالمَغْرِبِ. اللَّهُمَّ نَقِّنِي مِنْ خَطَايَايَ كَمَا يُنَقَّى الثَّوْبُ الأَبْيَضُ مِنَ الدَّنَسِ. اللَّهُمَّ اغْسِلْنِي مِنْ خَطَايَايَ بِالثَّلْجِ وَالمَاءِ وَالبَرَدِ»

«Allāhumma bāʿid baynī wa bayna khaṭāyāya kamā bāʿadta bayna-l-mashriqi wa-l-maghrib. Allāhumma naqqinī min khaṭāyāya kamā yunaqqa-th-thawbu-l-abyaḍu mina-d-danas. Allāhumma-ghsilnī min khaṭāyāya bi-th-thalji wa-l-māʾi wa-l-barad»

"O Allāh, distance me from my sins as You distanced the East from the West. O Allāh, cleanse me of my sins as a white garment is cleansed of dirt. O Allāh, wash me of my sins with snow, water, and hail."

كَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَقُولُهُ بَعْدَ التَّكْبِيرِمُتَّفَقٌ عَلَيْه · Bukhārī & Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Three vivid images of purity — distance, cleansing, washing — show how seriously a believer takes sin and how completely they trust Allāh to remove it. You begin the prayer admitting you arrive stained, and ask to be made clean enough to stand before Him. Humility, not self-satisfaction, is the doorway into ṣalāh.
٣

«وَجَّهْتُ وَجْهِيَ لِلَّذِي فَطَرَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالأَرْضَ حَنِيفًا، وَمَا أَنَا مِنَ المُشْرِكِينَ. إِنَّ صَلَاتِي وَنُسُكِي وَمَحْيَايَ وَمَمَاتِي لِلَّهِ رَبِّ العَالَمِينَ، لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ، وَبِذَلِكَ أُمِرْتُ وَأَنَا مِنَ المُسْلِمِينَ. اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ المَلِكُ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ، أَنْتَ رَبِّي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ، ظَلَمْتُ نَفْسِي وَاعْتَرَفْتُ بِذَنْبِي، فَاغْفِرْ لِي ذُنُوبِي جَمِيعًا إِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ، وَاهْدِنِي لِأَحْسَنِ الأَخْلَاقِ لَا يَهْدِي لِأَحْسَنِهَا إِلَّا أَنْتَ، وَاصْرِفْ عَنِّي سَيِّئَهَا لَا يَصْرِفُ عَنِّي سَيِّئَهَا إِلَّا أَنْتَ، لَبَّيْكَ وَسَعْدَيْكَ، وَالخَيْرُ كُلُّهُ فِي يَدَيْكَ، وَالشَّرُّ لَيْسَ إِلَيْكَ، أَنَا بِكَ وَإِلَيْكَ، تَبَارَكْتَ وَتَعَالَيْتَ»

«Wajjahtu wajhiya li-lladhī faṭara-s-samāwāti wa-l-arḍa ḥanīfan wa mā anā mina-l-mushrikīn. Inna ṣalātī wa nusukī wa maḥyāya wa mamātī li-llāhi rabbi-l-ʿālamīn, lā sharīka lah, wa bi-dhālika umirtu wa anā mina-l-muslimīn. Allāhumma anta-l-maliku lā ilāha illā ant, anta rabbī wa anā ʿabduk, ẓalamtu nafsī wa-ʿtaraftu bi-dhanbī, fa-ghfir lī dhunūbī jamīʿan innahu lā yaghfiru-dh-dhunūba illā ant…»

"I have turned my face to the One who created the heavens and the earth, devoted to Him, and I am not of the polytheists. My prayer, my sacrifice, my living and my dying are for Allāh, Lord of the worlds; He has no partner… O Allāh, You are the King, none has the right to be worshipped but You; You are my Lord and I am Your servant; I have wronged myself and confessed my sin, so forgive me all my sins… guide me to the best of character… turn its evil away from me… all good is in Your hands… You are blessed and exalted."

كَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَقُولُهُ إِذَا قَامَ إِلَى الصَّلَاةِرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
This longest opening is a whole creed compressed into a duʿāʾ: pure monotheism ("my prayer, sacrifice, life and death are for Allāh"), then total servanthood ("I have wronged myself"), then four requests — forgiveness, guidance to the best character, protection from the worst, and the affirmation that "all good is in Your hands, and evil is not attributed to You." To say it with presence is to re-enter Islam from its first principle every single prayer.

Item 11 — Whispering (Waswasah) in Prayer & Recitation
الحَدِيثُ — اِحْفَظْ وَاعْمَلْ

Workbook pp.33–34 — the Prophet's ﷺ remedy for the devil who disrupts prayer.

١

شَكَا عُثْمَانُ بْنُ أَبِي العَاصِ ﷺ أَنَّ الشَّيْطَانَ حَالَ بَيْنَهُ وَبَيْنَ صَلَاتِهِ وَقِرَاءَتِهِ، فَقَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ: «ذَاكَ شَيْطَانٌ يُقَالُ لَهُ: خِنْزَبٌ، فَإِذَا أَحْسَسْتَهُ فَتَعَوَّذْ بِاللَّهِ مِنْهُ، وَاتْفِلْ عَلَى يَسَارِكَ ثَلَاثًا» — قَالَ: فَفَعَلْتُ ذَلِكَ فَأَذْهَبَهُ اللَّهُ عَنِّي

«Dhāka shayṭānun yuqālu lah: Khinzab, fa-idhā aḥsastahu fa-taʿawwadh bi-llāhi minh, wa-tfil ʿalā yasārika thalāthan» — qāla: fa-faʿaltu dhālika fa-adhhabahu-llāhu ʿannī

ʿUthmān ibn Abi-l-ʿĀṣ complained that Satan was coming between him and his prayer and recitation. The Prophet ﷺ said: "That is a devil called Khinzab. When you sense him, seek refuge in Allāh from him and spit lightly to your left three times." He said: I did that, and Allāh removed it from me.

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Distraction in prayer is named for what it is — an enemy with a name, not a personal failing to despair over. The cure is active, not passive: the moment you notice the whisper, you take refuge in Allāh and physically turn from it. The lesson for the chronically distracted: don't fight waswasah with frustration; answer it with seeking refuge, then return to the prayer — and trust that Allāh removes what He commands you to flee.

📖 التَّفْل = a light puff of breath with a little spittle · يُلْبِسُهَا عَلَيَّ = he confuses my prayer and blocks my khushūʿ.

Item 12 — The Bowing (Rukūʿ)
الأَذْكَارُ — اِحْفَظْ وَاعْمَلْ

Workbook pp.35–36 — five authentic forms said while bowing. Vary between them.

١

«سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ العَظِيمِ»

«Subḥāna rabbiya-l-ʿaẓīm»

"Glory be to my Lord, the Most Great." (said three times or more)

كَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَقُولُهُ فِي رُكُوعِهِرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
The body bows low while the tongue declares Allāh "the Most Great" — posture and words agree. Bowing is the limbs' confession of His greatness; saying al-ʿAẓīm here matches your lowness to His loftiness. Worship is most whole when heart, body, and tongue say the same thing at once.
٢

«اللَّهُمَّ لَكَ رَكَعْتُ، وَبِكَ آمَنْتُ، وَلَكَ أَسْلَمْتُ، خَشَعَ لَكَ سَمْعِي، وَبَصَرِي، وَمُخِّي، وَعَظْمِي، وَعَصَبِي»

«Allāhumma laka rakaʿtu, wa bika āmantu, wa laka aslamtu, khashaʿa laka samʿī, wa baṣarī, wa mukhkhī, wa ʿaẓmī, wa ʿaṣabī»

"O Allāh, to You I have bowed, in You I have believed, to You I have submitted; my hearing, my sight, my brain, my bone and my nerves are humbled before You."

كَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَقُولُهُ إِذَا رَكَعَرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Submission is itemised down to the senses, the brain, the very bones and nerves — nothing of you is held back. The dhikr trains a whole-self surrender: it is not enough for the body to bow if the mind and faculties are roaming. Naming each part is a way of gathering all of yourself into the bow.
٣

«سُبْحَانَكَ اللَّهُمَّ رَبَّنَا وَبِحَمْدِكَ، اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِي»

«Subḥānaka-llāhumma rabbanā wa bi-ḥamdik, Allāhumma-ghfir lī»

"Glory be to You, O Allāh our Lord, and praise; O Allāh, forgive me."

كَانَ يُكْثِرُ مِنْهُ فِي رُكُوعِهِ وَسُجُودِهِمُتَّفَقٌ عَلَيْه · Bukhārī & Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
ʿĀʾishah said he recited this often, acting on the Qurʾān's command "so glorify the praise of your Lord and seek His forgiveness." It is a small window into how the Prophet ﷺ turned revelation directly into devotion — pairing glorification with a plea for forgiveness, the two wings of worship.
٤

«سُبُّوحٌ، قُدُّوسٌ، رَبُّ المَلَائِكَةِ وَالرُّوحِ»

«Subbūḥun, quddūsun, rabbu-l-malāʾikati wa-r-rūḥ»

"Perfect in glory, perfect in holiness, Lord of the angels and the Spirit (Jibrīl)."

كَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَقُولُهُ فِي رُكُوعِهِ وَسُجُودِهِرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Two intensive names — Subbūḥ (utterly free of every flaw) and Quddūs (utterly pure of all that is unworthy of Him) — declare Allāh's perfection, then His lordship over the highest creatures, the angels and Jibrīl. Bowing before the Lord of the angels reminds you who you are addressing: the One the angels themselves serve.
٥

«سُبْحَانَ ذِي الجَبَرُوتِ وَالمَلَكُوتِ وَالكِبْرِيَاءِ وَالعَظَمَةِ»

«Subḥāna dhi-l-jabarūti wa-l-malakūti wa-l-kibriyāʾi wa-l-ʿaẓamah»

"Glory be to the Possessor of absolute power, sovereignty, pride, and greatness."

كَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَقُولُهُ فِي رُكُوعِهِ وَسُجُودِهِرَوَاهُ أَبُو دَاوُد · Abū Dāwūd

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Four overwhelming attributes are stacked together — might, dominion, pride, greatness — all belonging to Allāh alone. Pride (kibriyāʾ) is praiseworthy only for Him; in a servant it is a disease. So bowing while reciting this is also a quiet cure for arrogance: you give all greatness back to its sole Owner.

Item 13 — Rising from the Bowing
الأَذْكَارُ — اِحْفَظْ وَاعْمَلْ

Workbook pp.37–38 — what the imām and the worshipper say on rising, and the praise after it.

١

يَقُولُ الإِمَامُ وَالمُنْفَرِدُ عِنْدَ الرَّفْعِ: «سَمِعَ اللَّهُ لِمَنْ حَمِدَهُ»، ثُمَّ يَقُولُ: «رَبَّنَا وَلَكَ الحَمْدُ، حَمْدًا كَثِيرًا طَيِّبًا مُبَارَكًا فِيهِ»

«Samiʿa-llāhu li-man ḥamidah» — thumma: «Rabbanā wa laka-l-ḥamd, ḥamdan kathīran ṭayyiban mubārakan fīh»

"Allāh hears whoever praises Him," then: "Our Lord, and to You is all praise — abundant, good, and blessed praise." (A man said it behind the Prophet ﷺ, who saw thirty-odd angels racing to be first to record it.)

عَنِ النَّبِيِّ ﷺرَوَاهُ البُخَارِيّ · al-Bukhārī

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
A single sincere phrase of praise set thirty-some angels competing to write it down — proof that the words we treat as routine are weighed and treasured above. The lesson: never belittle a small dhikr. Heaven may be racing over a sentence you almost said absent-mindedly.

«رَبَّنَا لَكَ الحَمْدُ، مِلْءَ السَّمَوَاتِ وَالأَرْضِ، وَمِلْءَ مَا شِئْتَ مِنْ شَيْءٍ بَعْدُ، أَهْلَ الثَّنَاءِ وَالمَجْدِ، أَحَقُّ مَا قَالَ العَبْدُ، وَكُلُّنَا لَكَ عَبْدٌ، اللَّهُمَّ لَا مَانِعَ لِمَا أَعْطَيْتَ، وَلَا مُعْطِيَ لِمَا مَنَعْتَ، وَلَا يَنْفَعُ ذَا الجَدِّ مِنْكَ الجَدُّ»

«Rabbanā laka-l-ḥamd, milʾa-s-samāwāti wa-l-arḍ, wa milʾa mā shiʾta min shayʾin baʿd, ahla-th-thanāʾi wa-l-majd, aḥaqqu mā qāla-l-ʿabd, wa kullunā laka ʿabd, Allāhumma lā māniʿa li-mā aʿṭayt, wa lā muʿṭiya li-mā manaʿt, wa lā yanfaʿu dha-l-jaddi minka-l-jadd»

"Our Lord, to You is praise — filling the heavens and the earth, and filling whatever else You will. O Worthy of praise and majesty! The truest thing a servant has said — and we are all Your servants. O Allāh, none can withhold what You give, none can give what You withhold, and the fortune of the fortunate avails him nothing against You."

كَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَقُولُهُ إِذَا رَفَعَ رَأْسَهُ مِنَ الرُّكُوعِرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
The praise expands to fill the heavens and earth, then collapses into pure dependence: "none withholds what You give, none gives what You withhold." No wealth, status or "luck" can rescue a person against Allāh's decree — only faith and obedience can. Standing upright after the bow, you are taught that all provision and all protection run through one Hand.

Item 14 — The Prostration (Sujūd)
الأَذْكَارُ — اِحْفَظْ وَاعْمَلْ

Workbook pp.39–40 — three forms said in the prostration, the closest a servant is to his Lord.

١

«سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الأَعْلَى»

«Subḥāna rabbiya-l-aʿlā»

"Glory be to my Lord, the Most High." (said three times or more)

كَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَقُولُهُ فِي سُجُودِهِرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
At your lowest physical point — forehead on the ground — you call Him "the Most High." The contrast is the whole meaning of sujūd: the more you lower yourself, the higher you declare Him, and the closer you come. The Prophet ﷺ said the servant is nearest to his Lord in prostration — so this is the place to pour out duʿāʾ.
٢

«اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِي ذَنْبِي كُلَّهُ، دِقَّهُ وَجِلَّهُ، وَأَوَّلَهُ وَآخِرَهُ، وَعَلَانِيَتَهُ وَسِرَّهُ»

«Allāhumma-ghfir lī dhanbī kullah, diqqahu wa jillah, wa awwalahu wa ākhirah, wa ʿalāniyatahu wa sirrah»

"O Allāh, forgive me all my sin — its small and its great, its first and its last, its open and its hidden."

كَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَقُولُهُ فِي سُجُودِهِرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Forgiveness is asked for in every dimension at once — size, time, and visibility — so that nothing slips through. It quietly teaches that real sins are not only the public, obvious ones; the small, the old, and the hidden all need covering. To beg for "all of it" is to refuse to make peace with any of it.
٣

«اللَّهُمَّ لَكَ سَجَدْتُ، وَبِكَ آمَنْتُ، وَلَكَ أَسْلَمْتُ، سَجَدَ وَجْهِي لِلَّذِي خَلَقَهُ وَصَوَّرَهُ، وَشَقَّ سَمْعَهُ وَبَصَرَهُ، تَبَارَكَ اللَّهُ أَحْسَنُ الخَالِقِينَ»

«Allāhumma laka sajadtu, wa bika āmantu, wa laka aslamtu, sajada wajhiya li-lladhī khalaqahu wa ṣawwarah, wa shaqqa samʿahu wa baṣarah, tabāraka-llāhu aḥsanu-l-khāliqīn»

"O Allāh, to You I have prostrated, in You I have believed, to You I have submitted; my face has prostrated to the One who created and shaped it, and opened its hearing and sight — blessed is Allāh, the best of creators."

كَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَقُولُهُ إِذَا سَجَدَرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
The face — the most honoured, expressive part of you — is laid down before "the One who created and shaped it." You acknowledge that even the senses you are using to worship (hearing, sight) were given, not earned. Prostration becomes an act of returning the gift to its Giver: nothing of you is self-made, so nothing of you is withheld.

Item 15 — The Tashahhud
الأَذْكَارُ — اِحْفَظْ وَاعْمَلْ

Workbook pp.41–42 — the testimony said in the sitting, and the blessings upon the Prophet ﷺ.

١

«التَّحِيَّاتُ لِلَّهِ، وَالصَّلَوَاتُ، وَالطَّيِّبَاتُ. السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكَ أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ وَرَحْمَةُ اللَّهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ. السَّلَامُ عَلَيْنَا وَعَلَى عِبَادِ اللَّهِ الصَّالِحِينَ. أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ»

«At-taḥiyyātu li-llāh, wa-ṣ-ṣalawātu, wa-ṭ-ṭayyibāt. As-salāmu ʿalayka ayyuha-n-nabiyyu wa raḥmatu-llāhi wa barakātuh. As-salāmu ʿalaynā wa ʿalā ʿibādi-llāhi-ṣ-ṣāliḥīn. Ashhadu an lā ilāha illa-llāh, wa ashhadu anna Muḥammadan ʿabduhu wa rasūluh»

"All greetings of veneration, prayers and good deeds are for Allāh. Peace be upon you, O Prophet, and the mercy of Allāh and His blessings. Peace be upon us and upon the righteous servants of Allāh. I bear witness that none has the right to be worshipped but Allāh, and I bear witness that Muḥammad is His servant and Messenger."

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺمُتَّفَقٌ عَلَيْه · Bukhārī & Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
The tashahhud opens by handing all veneration, all prayer, all good back to Allāh — a refusal to let any worship leak toward another. Then it spreads peace outward: to the Prophet ﷺ, to oneself, and to every righteous servant of Allāh on earth and in heaven. To mean it is to wish well to the whole believing community in every prayer.
٢

«اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَى آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ، كَمَا صَلَّيْتَ عَلَى إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَى آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ. اللَّهُمَّ بَارِكْ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَى آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ، كَمَا بَارَكْتَ عَلَى إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَى آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ، إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ»

«Allāhumma ṣalli ʿalā Muḥammadin wa ʿalā āli Muḥammad, kamā ṣallayta ʿalā Ibrāhīma wa ʿalā āli Ibrāhīm, innaka ḥamīdun majīd. Allāhumma bārik ʿalā Muḥammadin wa ʿalā āli Muḥammad, kamā bārakta ʿalā Ibrāhīma wa ʿalā āli Ibrāhīm, innaka ḥamīdun majīd»

"O Allāh, send blessings upon Muḥammad and the family of Muḥammad as You blessed Ibrāhīm and the family of Ibrāhīm; You are Praiseworthy, Glorious. O Allāh, bless Muḥammad and the family of Muḥammad as You blessed Ibrāhīm and the family of Ibrāhīm; You are Praiseworthy, Glorious."

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺمُتَّفَقٌ عَلَيْه · Bukhārī & Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
"Ṣalli ʿalā" means "O Allāh, praise him in the highest assembly (of angels)." You cannot raise the Prophet's ﷺ rank yourself, so you ask Allāh to do it — and the reward returns to you tenfold. Linking him to Ibrāhīm ties the final Messenger to the father of prophets, placing your own prayer inside one unbroken line of pure monotheism.

Item 16 — The Supplication Before the Salām
الأَذْكَارُ — اِحْفَظْ وَاعْمَلْ

Workbook pp.43–44 — what to seek refuge from and ask for in the last sitting, before giving salām.

١

«اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ عَذَابِ جَهَنَّمَ، وَمِنْ عَذَابِ القَبْرِ، وَمِنْ فِتْنَةِ المَحْيَا وَالمَمَاتِ، وَمِنْ شَرِّ فِتْنَةِ المَسِيحِ الدَّجَّالِ»

«Allāhumma innī aʿūdhu bika min ʿadhābi jahannam, wa min ʿadhābi-l-qabr, wa min fitnati-l-maḥyā wa-l-mamāt, wa min sharri fitnati-l-masīḥi-d-dajjāl»

"O Allāh, I seek refuge in You from the punishment of Hell, from the punishment of the grave, from the trial of life and death, and from the evil of the trial of the False Messiah (Dajjāl)."

قَالَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ — يُقَالُ بَعْدَ التَّشَهُّدِرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
The Prophet ﷺ commanded seeking refuge from these four before salām — a curriculum of the soul's greatest dangers: the final punishment, the hidden one in the grave, the trials of this life and the moment of death, and the supreme deception of the end of time. Naming your real enemies, daily, keeps the heart sober and dependent.
٢

«اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ الجُبْنِ، وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ أَنْ أُرَدَّ إِلَى أَرْذَلِ العُمُرِ، وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ فِتْنَةِ الدُّنْيَا، وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ عَذَابِ القَبْرِ»

«Allāhumma innī aʿūdhu bika mina-l-jubn, wa aʿūdhu bika an uradda ilā ardhali-l-ʿumur, wa aʿūdhu bika min fitnati-d-dunyā, wa aʿūdhu bika min ʿadhābi-l-qabr»

"O Allāh, I seek refuge in You from cowardice, from being returned to the most feeble old age, from the trial of this world, and from the punishment of the grave."

كَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَتَعَوَّذُ بِهِرَوَاهُ البُخَارِيّ · al-Bukhārī

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
Even the Prophet ﷺ sought refuge from cowardice and senility — reminders that a believer wants a life of courage and a mind that stays useful to its last day. It teaches a wholesome ambition: not merely to avoid Hell, but to be spared a weak, fruitless ending, and to keep serving Allāh with strength and clarity for as long as you live.
٣

«اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِي مَا قَدَّمْتُ وَمَا أَخَّرْتُ، وَمَا أَسْرَرْتُ وَمَا أَعْلَنْتُ، وَمَا أَسْرَفْتُ، وَمَا أَنْتَ أَعْلَمُ بِهِ مِنِّي. أَنْتَ المُقَدِّمُ وَأَنْتَ المُؤَخِّرُ، لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ»

«Allāhumma-ghfir lī mā qaddamtu wa mā akhkhart, wa mā asrartu wa mā aʿlant, wa mā asraftu, wa mā anta aʿlamu bihi minnī. Anta-l-muqaddimu wa anta-l-muʾakhkhir, lā ilāha illā ant»

"O Allāh, forgive me what I have sent ahead and what I left behind, what I concealed and what I made public, my excesses, and what You know better than I. You are the One who brings forward and the One who puts back; none has the right to be worshipped but You."

كَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ يَقُولُهُ بَيْنَ التَّشَهُّدِ وَالتَّسْلِيمِرَوَاهُ مُسْلِم · Muslim

💎 الفَائِدَة · Deeper lesson
A breathtakingly complete request for pardon — past and future, secret and open, the sins you remember and "what You know better than I." It leaves no corner of the self unsurrendered. Ending with "You bring forward and put back" hands even the timing of your life and provision to Him: total forgiveness sought, total control acknowledged.
